### **Script: "Traditional Industry Bosses Want to Use AI? First Avoid These Three Fatal 'Pitfalls'!"**

**(Video style: narration + keyword/scene animation assists, moderate pace, tone like sharing insights between friends)**

---

**【0-15 seconds: Opening Hook, Subvert Cognition】**

(Facing the camera, slightly "revealing secrets" feeling)

"When many traditional industry bosses talk AI transformation with me, they open with three sentences: 'We don't have enough data,' 'Technology is too difficult,' 'Budget is limited.' But honestly, **these aren't the most critical issues.**"

(Pause briefly)

"What's really causing AI transformation failures and wasting money are three more hidden, more common 'cognitive pitfalls.' Today I'm going to burst this bubble for you, especially the third point—90% of bosses have fallen into it."

**【15-50 seconds: Pain Point One — "Magic Wand" Illusion】**

"The first pitfall, I call it the **'Magic Wand' Illusion.**"

"Many bosses imagine AI as a wishing pool: 'I have a pain point, buy a set of AI systems, next month efficiency doubles, costs halve.' This is like buying the most expensive brushes and thinking you'll become Picasso. **AI isn't a finished product, it's paints and brushes.**"

"What's the real pain point? It's **you don't know what exactly you want to paint.** What you need is to break down this problem of 'inaccurate inventory backlog warnings' into: need to analyze which data from the past 3 years, what are the warning rules, do you want AI to ultimately give you a report or an alert... without this 'painting concept,' even the most expensive AI is just decoration."

**【50 seconds-1:25: Pain Point Two — "Data Ruins" Dilemma】**

"The second pitfall, more common, called the **'Data Ruins' Dilemma.**"

"Many companies say 'All our data is on the computer!' But data from the past decade might be scattered across 8 Excel sheets, 3 different versions of ERP systems, even sales people's WeChat chat records. This data has inconsistent formats, chaotic standards, lots of errors."

"This is like wanting to refine gold from a gold mine, but finding the mine stacked with unsorted ore, dirt, even garbage. **AI is a top-tier alchemist, but not a garbage sorter.** If you let AI learn directly from it, it will only learn your 'chaos.' So the first investment and biggest resistance in transformation often isn't buying AI, but going back to **painfully organize and unify your accumulated 'data ruins.'**"

**【1:25-end: Pain Point Three — "Organizational Permafrost Layer"】**

"The third pitfall, deepest and most fatal, I call it the **'Organizational Permafrost Layer.'**"

"This has nothing to do with technology, it has to do with people. You spent tremendous effort deploying an AI system and find it completely stuck. Why? Middle management fears it makes them seem unimportant, employees fear it takes their jobs, old-timers think 'we did fine the old way.'"

"**Technology freezes fast, but organizations are soil frozen for over a decade.** AI doesn't come to assist people, it comes to change work flows and power structures. If you can't preemptively resolve this fear, can't shift the narrative from 'AI replaces you' to 'AI makes you stronger, lets you do more valuable things,' then any transformation will hit its head bloody on this 'permafrost.'"

**【End: Summary and Call to Action】**

"So you see, transforming traditional business, the hardest part isn't touching the future, it's **sorting the past, defining the present, and warming hearts.**"

"If you're an entrepreneur or manager interested in AI, **follow me.** I won't just talk about flashy AI tech with you, I'll help you break down these underlying logics about 'people' and 'cognition' that truly determine success or failure. Next episode, let's talk about how to use the smallest cost to start your first AI verification experiment and avoid these pitfalls."
