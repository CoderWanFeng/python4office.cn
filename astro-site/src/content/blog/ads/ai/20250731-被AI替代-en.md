No, saying "all" knowledge work will be replaced by AI is **hyperbole**. A more accurate judgment is:  
> **AI will "partially replace" or even "mostly replace" the **repetitive, standardized, rule-based** portions of knowledge work, but humans remain irreplaceable** in scenarios requiring **creativity, emotional interaction, value judgment, and cross-domain integration**.

---

### 1. Which Knowledge Jobs Will Be **Replaced Earliest**?
- **High repetitiveness**: Data entry, basic reports, contract clause comparison  
- **High standardization**: Junior accounting entries, legal document templates, insurance underwriting rules  
- **High rule-based**: Customer service FAQ, code completion, medical image initial screening  
McKinsey calls such tasks "automatable work activities," predicting they will cover 50% of total working hours by around 2045.

---

### 2. Which Knowledge Jobs Are **Temporarily Safe**?
| Dimension | Case | Why Humans Are Irreplaceable |
|---|---|---|
| **Creativity** | Scientific hypothesis generation, advertising creativity, screenplay writing | Requires "eureka moments" that jump outside data distributions |
| **Emotional Interaction** | Psychological counseling, complex negotiations, brand PR | Depends on empathy, trust-building |
| **Value Judgment** | Ethical review, strategic trade-offs, public policy | Involves culture, ethics, interest games |
| **Cross-Domain Integration** | Product manager, CTO, entrepreneur | Connecting technology, market, organization, and capital into a closed loop |

---

### 3. Most Likely Form in the Next 10 Years: **Human-AI Collaboration**
- **Programmers**: AI writes 80% of code, humans do architecture and requirement insights  
- **Doctors**: AI does initial image screening, humans do comprehensive diagnosis and patient communication  
- **Teachers**: AI handles knowledge point推送 and homework grading, humans lead heuristic discussions and emotional companionship  

---

### 4. How to Make Yourself **Hard to Replace**?
1. **Upgrade "AI-Unscorable Abilities"**: Critical thinking, cross-disciplinary insight, leadership  
2. **Become a "Conductor of AI"**: Master prompt engineering, data interpretation, process orchestration  
3. **Deepen "Human Interfaces"**: Sales, negotiation, coaching, psychological counseling - jobs requiring trust and emotion  

---

### 5. Conclusion
> AI is not a flood beast, but a **"knowledge lever"**.  
> **Levers only amplify ability, they don't replace the person who knows how to use them.**

Rather than worrying about "being replaced," think about this: **How to position yourself at the long end of the lever, not the end that gets pried off.**
