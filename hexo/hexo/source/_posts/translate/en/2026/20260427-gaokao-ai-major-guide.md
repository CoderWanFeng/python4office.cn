The college entrance exam countdown has begun.

Once scores come out, the most headache-inducing thing isn't the exam — it's **filling out the application**.

Choose a city? A school? Or a major?

Especially this year, with the AI boom, many parents and students are asking: **Should we apply for AI-related majors?**

As a senior who switched from a law student to a programmer and is now all-in on AI, I'm going to make this clear today.

---

## The Bottom Line First

**AI majors are worth applying for, but it depends on the situation.**

Not everyone is suitable, and not every school is reliable.

Below, I'll give you a decision framework.

---

## 1. Choose a City: Highest Priority

My view: **City > School > Major.**

Why?

Because where you go to college, you'll most likely intern, work, and even settle down in that city.

Cities where the AI industry is most developed:

**If you can go to a tier-1 city, go to a tier-1 city.**

If you really can't, at least go to a provincial capital.

There are more AI internship opportunities, more campus recruitment positions, and completely different access to resources.

---

## 2. Choose a School: Avoid These Pitfalls

There's a core principle for choosing a school: **If you can get into a 985/211, go for it.**

But if you can't, watch out for these categories:

### ❌ Pitfall 1: "AI" Branded Diploma Mills

Some schools have newly opened "Artificial Intelligence" majors, but:

- No reliable faculty
- No decent labs
- Course systems are haphazardly assembled

This kind of major sounds good but doesn't teach you the real stuff.

**How to judge:** Check whether this major has an independent department, master's/doctoral programs, and industry-partnered labs.

### ❌ Pitfall 2: AI Majors That Only Teach You How to Tune Parameters

The AI industry changes too fast.

TensorFlow taught today might be unused next year.

What's truly valuable is: **Mathematical foundation + Programming ability + Problem-solving mindset.**

If an AI major only teaches you "how to use AI tools" without teaching you "why AI is designed this way," be cautious.

### ✅ Recommended: Choose "Computer Science + X" Rather Than Pure "AI"

What does this mean?

**Computer Science** + any industry direction is more stable than just studying "Artificial Intelligence."

For example:

- Computer Science + Medicine = Medical AI
- Computer Science + Finance = FinTech
- Computer Science + Law = Legal Tech

AI is a tool, not a goal.

**Master the underlying ability, and you can stand firm in any industry.**

---

## 3. Choose a Major: 4 Safest Directions

### Direction 1: Computer Science and Technology (Most Stable)

**Suitable for:** Students with sufficient scores who want to play it safe

**Why recommend:**
- A century-old major with a mature curriculum
- Widest employment scope: internet, finance, healthcare, education
- AI is just a branch of computer science. Master the basics, and AI follows naturally

**Salary reference:** Fresh graduates 8k–20k CNY, higher in tier-1 cities

---

### Direction 2: Data Science and Big Data Technology (Rising Star)

**Suitable for:** Students good at math who enjoy analyzing data

**Why recommend:**
- Data position shortage remains large in 2026
- In the era of large models, data is the core asset
- Wide application range, from e-commerce recommendation to medical diagnosis

**Core skills:** Python, statistics, machine learning basics, SQL

**Salary reference:** Fresh graduates 10k–25k CNY

---

### Direction 3: Intelligent Science and Technology (Advanced)

**Suitable for:** Students with high scores who want to deep-dive into AI

**Why recommend:**
- More AI-focused than computer science
- Courses cover machine learning, deep learning, natural language processing
- If you pursue a master's or doctorate, the salary ceiling is extremely high

---

## 4. Final Word

Choosing a major isn't choosing what's hottest, but choosing what's most stable for you.

In the AI era, foundational skills matter most:

- Mathematical thinking
- Programming ability
- Problem-solving mindset

These abilities will serve you for a lifetime, regardless of how the AI industry evolves.

**Don't just chase trends; build foundations.**
