Why Are You Busier but Poorer? 'Economics of Small Islands' Gives 3 Harsh Truths for Side Hustlers

发表于2025-11-04|更新于2026-04-28
|阅读量:

👉 Project Official Website: https://www.python-office.com/ 👈

👉 Open source project exchange group 👈

Recently reading "Economics of Small Islands," there's an important story at the beginning: On an island where only raw fish could be eaten, Eber, Becker, and Charlie repeated the "catch fish-eat fish-sleep" cycle every day. Until one day, Eber made a fate-changing decision - to weave a net even while hungry. When Becker and Charlie laughed at him "Are you crazy? Starving for a day for a crappy net?" no one expected this net would completely rewrite the island's economic destiny.

The next day, Eber easily caught two fish with his newly woven net. This simple story hides the complete secret of making money from side hustles: All seemingly "getting something for nothing" gains come from the early "starving while weaving nets" investment. Today we use this story to break down the pitfalls ordinary people most easily fall into when doing side hustles. At the end of the article, there are 3 "Net Weaving Guides" you can directly apply 💰

Harsh Truth of Making Money from Side Hustles: You're Fishing, Others are Weaving Nets

Zero Investment to Make Money? Just Selling Time in a Different Way

"3 hours a day, earn over 10k/month" "Make money at home with one phone" - Are these headlines familiar? But reality is, all "zero investment" side hustles are essentially selling your time, and at a discount.

Like island residents catching fish by hand, seems "zero cost," but actually repeating inefficient labor every day. Those "typing part-time" and "like for money" spammed on friend circles are essentially no different from catching fish by hand: trading time for small money, forever stuck in the "catch one, eat one" predicament.

35-year-old Lin Wan knows this pain best. Before having a child, she was an office worker, did PPT commissioned work, Taobao order brushing, even helped people queue for hospital registration after work. Busy until midnight every day, monthly income at most 3000 yuan. Until one day she saw the story in "Economics of Small Islands" and suddenly woke up: "I'm busy 'catching fish' every day, but never spent time 'weaving a net'."

3-Month Net Weaving Period: From 3k to 8k Monthly Income Transformation

In March 2024, Lin Wan made a decision: pause all scattered part-time work, spend 3 months systematically learning self-media operations. Every day after putting the child to sleep, she learned CapCut until midnight, turning the living room into a "temporary studio," practicing on-camera delivery with a phone stand.

First month, she posted 20+ videos, highest views only a few thousand. But she didn't give up, watching data发现: content about storage tips got no views, sharing "30 minutes to complete kitchen deep cleaning" had明显 higher interaction rate. So she adjusted direction, focusing on "low-cost, easy-to-operate" home scenarios: using old clothes to make storage bags, 100 yuan to transform a children's room corner, publishing family account books to teach everyone money-saving.

Turning point appeared in the 3rd month. A video "200 yuan to handle one week's dinner for family of three" suddenly went viral, views over 1 million, gained 30k followers in a single day. Now Lin Wan relies on ads, sales, and storage consulting, monthly stable income 8000+, key is time freedom, can read picture books with child 📚

Programmer's AI Fishing Net: 10 Days Development, 250k Monthly Income

If Lin Wan's story isn't shocking enough, programmer Samuel's case will make you re-understand the power of "net weaving." This ordinary programmer developed 3 apps using AI tools, **now earns $35,000 USD monthly (about 250k RMB)**躺着赚钱.

His secret is simple: Don't innovate, only do "1% optimization". Like discovering overseas "quitting habits" demand is strong, used AI to generate product architecture, write code, make interfaces, launched one app in 10 days. The best part is that "face temperature detection" feature - seems high-tech, actually calling AI interface to implement.

Samuel's 3 apps have no complex technology, but perfectly illustrate Eber's wisdom: Nets themselves can't be eaten, but can help you catch more fish. Like his developed automation scripts, earning him money 24/7, while most people are still manually "fishing."
