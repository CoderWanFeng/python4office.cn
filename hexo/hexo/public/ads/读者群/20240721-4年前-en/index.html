An Article I Wrote 4 Years Ago Got Paid Again Today!


Hey everyone, this is 晚枫 (Wanfeng). Today I want to share something interesting: an article I wrote 4 years ago got paid for again today!

The Beginning

I started doing programming content in 2018. Since I transitioned from law school to become a Python programmer, I understand the struggles of beginners — especially those outside the tech field — better than most. That's why from the start, I focused on sharing practical Python beginner knowledge and real-world projects.

Initially, all my tutorials were free. I'd stay up late every night after work to update content, and kept that up for over a year. Eventually, work got busier, more people reached out for consulting, and doing it purely out of passion became unsustainable. I switched to a model where tech sharing stayed free, but original courses and Q&A groups became paid.

The article that got paid today was actually my first Q&A group, priced at just 1 yuan back then: link here 👉 Python Automation Office Community · Discussion Group

Rewards

What have I gained from running these Q&A groups and original courses?

I think the most important thing is having a long-term career worth building — calling it a business wouldn't be an exaggeration.

From the very beginning of doing content creation, I saw this as something worth building for the long haul. Good work (articles, videos) I create can completely belong to me and generate ongoing returns. Unlike working for someone else, where late nights and overtime produce work that belongs to your boss — you get nothing but a salary and experience.

As a long-term project, I have enough time to refine it. I understand more and more what my fans want and what a reasonable price is for them. I can build a moat in the market without worrying about career crises.

Plus, as a content creator, the accumulation of followers has brought me collaboration opportunities I'd never have gotten as a pure programmer. For example, last year I helped plan and host Python China (Chongqing), plus technical promotions for major companies.

And finally, there's the income improvement — which is pretty self-explanatory. I now have 300,000+ followers across all platforms, and a single ad placement pays enough to cover my living expenses for a month.
