Major Companies Rush into Lobster Industry: Hype or Real Opportunity?

Some say it's hype, some say it's dimension reduction attack, some say it's a new paradigm for rural revitalization.

Today, I want to set aside emotions and talk about it with data and logic: Is major companies rushing into the lobster industry hype or real opportunity?

🎯 Major Companies' Real Intentions

Surface: Rural Revitalization and Agricultural Support

Official statements are consistent:

  • "Respond to national rural revitalization strategy"
  • "Empower traditional agriculture with technology"
  • "Help farmers increase income"

These words are not wrong, but not the whole story.

Deep: 4 Strategic Considerations

1. Seeking New Growth Curves

The internet industry has entered stock competition:

  • User growth hitting ceiling (internet penetration rate 75%)
  • Advertising revenue slowing (2025 growth only 8%)
  • Cloud service competition intensifying (price war continuing)

Lobster industry 450 billion yuan market, even if capturing only 5%, that's a 22.5 billion cake.

2. Polishing Vertical Scenario AI Capabilities

Lobster scenarios seem simple but actually cover core AI capabilities:

  • Computer vision (freshness detection)
  • Time series prediction (price fluctuations)
  • Decision optimization (procurement strategy)
  • Multi-modal understanding (image-text generation)

If you do lobster well, you can replicate to other agricultural products.

3. Seizing Policy Dividends

2026 Central Document No. 1 clearly proposes:

  • "Accelerate AI application in agriculture"
  • "Support tech companies participating in digital village construction"
  • "Provide tax benefits and subsidies for AI+ agriculture projects"

Policy window period, major companies won't miss it.

4. Building Ecological Barriers

Once a major company's lobster solution becomes industry standard:

  • Farmers depend on its technology
  • Distributors use its platform
  • Catering stores connect to its system

After ecological barriers form, latecomers have a hard time catching up.

⚖️ Is It Just Hype? My Judgment

Has hype elements, but not all

Hype parts:

  • Marketing propaganda is indeed exaggerated ("AI changes lobster industry")
  • Some features are flashy but impractical (like "lobster NFT")
  • Free quota limited, follow-up charging not transparent

Real parts:

  • Technical investment is real money (each at least 10 million level)
  • Real implementation cases exist (I've verified 5)
  • Policy subsidies are real and available (up to 40%)

My Conclusion

Short-term (1-2 years): Some hype exists, some projects will fail

Medium-term (3-5 years): Those with real technology and understanding of scenarios will survive

Long-term (5+ years): AI+ agriculture will become an independent track, lobster is the first verification scenario
