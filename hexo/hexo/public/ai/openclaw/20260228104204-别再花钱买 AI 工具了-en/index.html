After Spending 3000 Yuan on AI Tools, I Discovered This Free Open Source Treasure

发表于2026-02-28|更新于2026-04-28
|阅读量:

Hello everyone, I'm programmer Wan Feng.

Let me start with a harsh fact: In the past six months, I spent over 3000 yuan on AI tools.

ChatGPT Plus, Midjourney, various domestic AI model memberships... Every month when the automatic deduction happens, my heart bleeds.

What's even more infuriating—these tools aren't even good to use!

  • WeChat messages come in, have to manually copy to ChatGPT to ask
  • Want to automate something,，却发现 API prices are outrageous
  • Change platforms and have to pay again...

Until last week, a friend who does open source threw me a project link, saying: "Try this, it's free."

My first reaction was the same as yours: Can free stuff actually be any good?

After 3 days of use, I quietly cancelled 3 paid subscriptions.

It's Called OpenClaw—What Exactly Is It?

One sentence: An AI housekeeper that connects all your communication tools.

Imagine this scenario—

When you wake up in the morning, on your phone you have:

  • WeChat customer inquiries about products
  • Feishu colleagues pushing for reports
  • Urgent notices in DingTalk groups
  • Technical discussions on Telegram

My old self: frantically switching between apps, half the morning gone just like that.

Now with OpenClaw: One entry point, everything done.

It automatically helps you:

  • ✅ Reply to common questions (remembers your business knowledge)
  • ✅ Generate daily/weekly reports (directly sent to Feishu)
  • ✅ Monitor keyword alerts (important messages never missed)
  • ✅ Execute scheduled tasks (like sending weather + schedule every morning at 8)

Key point: Completely free, open source code, data stored on your own computer.

Why Is It Better Than Paid Tools?

1️⃣ True "All Platforms," Not Just Talk

Most AI assistants on the market only support one or two platforms.

OpenClaw currently supports: WeChat, Feishu, DingTalk, WeCom Work, WhatsApp, Telegram, Discord, Slack...

And it's still continuously adding more.

What does this mean?

Your workflow doesn't need to change to fit the tool. Instead, the tool adapts to your workflow.

2️⃣ It "Remembers" You, Getting Smarter Over Time

This is the feature that surprised me most.

On first use, I told it: "I'm in the Python training business, students often ask about installation issues."

The second time, when a student asked "how to install Python," it directly gave my usual response style.

The third time, it even added: "If you still have problems, you can ask Teacher Wan Feng for the installation video."

This isn't keyword matching—it's truly understanding context.

Like having an assistant who's been with you for three years, no need to explain from scratch every time.

3️⃣ Build Your Own Features, Don't Pay for Things You Don't Need

What's the biggest pitfall of paid tools?

Bundling sales.

You need feature A, but have to buy the package containing A+B+C+D, 199 yuan per month.

OpenClaw's approach is: Modular skill system.

Install what you need:

  • Check weather → Install weather skill
  • Watch stocks → Install stock-watcher skill
  • Write PPT → Install qwen-ppt skill
  • Even write your own skill, can be connected in 10 minutes

Install as much as you use, never waste anything.

4️⃣ Data Security, You Decide

Everyone who uses cloud AI tools knows—all your chat logs and business data are stored on others' servers.

OpenClaw is local deployment:

  • Code publicly available on GitHub, anyone can audit
  • Data stored on your own machine
  • Sensitive operations require authorization, never acts without permission

For friends in business, this point is extremely important.

Real Comparison: Where Does the Money Actually Save?

I did a simple calculation:

ItemPrevious Monthly CostNow
ChatGPT Plus$20Use free credits or self-host
Some domestic AI model membership¥68Don't need
Automation tool Zapier$20Built into OpenClaw

Over one year, saved nearly 5000 yuan.

And this doesn't even count the time cost savings.

Who Is OpenClaw Best For?

Strongly recommend these people try:

🎓 Students/freelancers —— Limited budget, but want AI for efficiency
💼 Office workers —— Lots of messages, lots of meetings, want liberation from repetitive work
💰 Small business owners —— Need automated customer service, order processing, but can't afford a tech team
🚀 Side hustle crowd —— One person handling multiple platforms, urgently need tool assistance
👨‍💻 Developers —— Want to read source code to learn, or build on it for secondary development

Might not be suitable:

❌ Expecting to lie down and make money after installation (tools are amplifiers, not money printers)
❌ Unwilling to read even a 10-minute tutorial (，确实没办法)
❌ Allergic to the word "open source" (出门右转买会员吧)
